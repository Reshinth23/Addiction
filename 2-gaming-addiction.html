Gaming Addiction: The Hidden Battle

What is Gaming Addiction?

Gaming addiction is a condition where individuals spend excessive time playing video games, to the detriment of their personal, social, or professional lives. Recognized by the World Health Organization (WHO), it can have serious consequences on mental and physical health.

Signs and Symptoms

Look out for these common signs:

  • Preoccupation with gaming, even when not playing
  • Increased time spent gaming over time
  • Neglecting personal hygiene and responsibilities
  • Withdrawal symptoms like irritability or sadness when not gaming

Statistics on Gaming Addiction

1 in 10 gamers may exhibit signs of gaming addiction, according to recent studies.

70% of gamers report gaming for more than 2 hours daily, which can lead to potential addiction.

Coping Strategies

Here are some strategies to manage gaming addiction:

  • Set a timer to limit gaming sessions
  • Engage in alternative hobbies or activities
  • Seek professional help if necessary
  • Establish a gaming schedule that includes breaks
